# Cred Bureau Rewards Review Rubric

## Category Point Ranges & Allocations

### 1. Matched-task Rewards (50% allocation)
**Point range:** 25-100 points per contribution  
**Criteria:**
- Completed a matched task from the Synagent task queue
- Evidence shows successful completion (screenshots, URLs, deliverables)
- Task complexity and impact considered
- **Base:** 25 points for basic completion
- **Tiered:** 50+ for complex tasks, 75+ for high-impact work, 100 for exceptional outcomes

### 2. Task Creation (15% allocation)  
**Point range:** 10-40 points per contribution  
**Criteria:**
- Created a well-defined task that others can complete
- Clear scope, deliverables, and success criteria
. - Tasks should be actionable and valuable to the ecosystem
-
- **Base:** 10 points for simple task creation
- **Tiered:** 25 for intermediate complexity, 40 for comprehensive task frameworks

### 3. Bug Reports and Friction Logs (15% allocation)
**Point range:** 10-60 points per contribution  
**Criteria:**
- Identified a bug in Synagent systems or workflows
- Documented reproduction steps clearly
- Suggested fix or workaround (bonus points)
- **Base:** 10 points for basic bug report
- **Tiered:** 30 for critical bugs with workarounds, 60 for security/blocking issues with fixes

### 4. Product-changing Feedback (10% allocation)
**Point range:** 10-50 points per contribution  
**Criteria:**
- Feedback that leads to or suggests product changes
-A Evidence of impact (design mockups, detailed analysis)
- **Base:** 10 points for constructive feedback
- **Tiered:** 30 for feedback with implementation suggestions, 50 for feedback that changes roadmap

### 5. Active Referrals (5% allocation)
**Point range:** 10-30 points per contribution  
**Criteria:**
- Referred an active participant who contributes meaningfully
- Both referrer and referred must have verifiable contributions
- **Base:** 10 points per successful referral
- **Tiered:** 20 for referrals with multiple contributions, 30 for referrals who become top contributors

### 6. Wildcard Grants (5% allocation, manual)
**Point range:** discretionary, manual allocation  
**Criteria:**
:
- Exceptional contributions not covered by other categories
- Community building, documentation, mentorship
- Reviewer discretion with anti-farm oversight

## Social Scoring Rubric (Supporting Evidence Only)

### Social Contribution Types & Base Points

1. **Original post/thread about Synagent** – 10 base points
   - Must be substantive content, not just links
   - Should demonstrate understanding of the project

2. **Quote post with real commentary** – 6 base points  
   - Not just a retweet or empty quote
   - Must add analysis, critique, or expansion

3. **Substantive reply** – 3 base points
   - Adds value to the conversation
   - Not just agreement/disagreement without reasoning

4. **Emoji-only/one-word/simple reply** – 0 points
   - Considered low-effort social
   - Does not count toward social score

### Quality Multiplier

Apply multiplier based on content quality:

-p **0× multiplier:** Low effort, generic content
- **1× multiplier:** Standard quality, meets basic criteria
- **1.5× multiplier:** Above average, insightful commentary
- **2× multiplier:** Exceptional content, changes perception

### Daily & Season Limits

- **Maximum 2 scored social contributions per participant per UTC day** (max 2 scored social contributions)
- **Social contributions capped at 15% of payout-eligible season score** (max 15%)
- Social cannot dominate matched-task, task-creation, bug/friction, or product-feedback work

## Evidence Quality Rules

### Acceptable Evidence
- **URLs:** Direct links to contributions (GitHub, X posts, task completion proofs)
- **Screenshots:** Clear, timestamped, showing relevant context
- **Video:** Short clips demonstrating functionality (≤2 minutes)
- **Documentation:** Written reports, analysis, or mockups

### Unacceptable Evidence
- **Blurry/low-quality media**
- **Links to private content** (behind login/paywalls)
- **Generic praise without specific context**
- **Duplicate submissions** across multiple categories

## Duplicate/Spam Handling

### Automatic Rejection
- Same contribution submitted multiple times
- Content copied from other participants
- **Evidence of farming** (coordinated low-effort submissions)

### Manual Review Required
- Similar but distinct contributions
- Edge cases where intent is unclear
- Participants with history of quality contributions

## Conflict Notes

### When to Document Conflicts
- Reviewer has personal relationship with participant
- Reviewer has financial interest in participant's success
- Reviewer has previously collaborated with participant
- Any situation where impartiality could be questioned

### Conflict Documentation Format
```
Conflict: [Description of relationship]
Action: [How conflict managed - e.g., another reviewer assigned]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
```

## Payout Eligibility Marking (payout eligible)

### Mark payout-eligible FALSE when:
1. **Evidence quality issues** – insufficient proof of contribution
2. **Suspected farming** – patterns of low-effort submissions
3. **Duplicate submissions** – same work claimed multiple times
4. **Conflict of interest** – without proper mitigation
5. **Violation of social caps** – attempting to game system via social only

### Mark payout-eligible TRUE when:
1. **All evidence passes quality check**
2. **Anti-farm review checklist completed**
3. **No conflicts or conflicts properly managed**
4. **Social contributions within allowed caps**
5. **Category points assigned per rubric**

## Anti-farm Review Checklist

**Required before export:**

- [ ] Evidence URLs valid and accessible
- [ ] No duplicate submissions detected
- [ ] Social contributions ≤2 per day
- [ ] Social score ≤15% of total season score
- [ ] No patterns of coordinated low-effort submissions
- [ ] Conflict notes documented if applicable
- [ ] Reviewer confident contribution is genuine
- [ ] Points assigned per category rubric ranges
