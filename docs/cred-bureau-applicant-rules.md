# Cred Bureau Closed Beta Applicant Rules

Last updated: 2026-05-13 UTC
Status: locked for closed beta

## Approval mode

Closed beta approvals are manual only.

The rules below are reviewer guidance, not automatic approval logic. No applicant should be auto-approved during this beta round, even if they satisfy every baseline requirement. Automated approvals can be considered later after the team has enough reviewed applications, abuse patterns, and quality signals.

## Baseline requirements

Every applicant must have:

- A Helixa human profile URL.
- Telegram handle or email so the team can contact them manually.
- A clear reason for wanting Cred Bureau access.
- Enough profile or supporting-link context for a reviewer to understand who they are.

Applications that miss a baseline requirement should stay pending with reviewer notes unless the reviewer determines they are spam, fake, or bad-faith.

## Good-fit applicants

Closed beta should prioritize:

- Builders who can test Synagent or Helixa workflows seriously.
- Reviewers/operators who can evaluate agents, profiles, or trust signals.
- Founders or early agent users with credible use cases.
- Trusted community members who can give useful feedback.
- People with clear identity, contact, and context across Helixa profile, Telegram/email, LinkedIn, website, or portfolio.

## Pending / parked cases

Park as pending when:

- The Helixa profile exists but is thin or incomplete.
- Contact info exists but the reviewer needs more context.
- The applicant might be useful, but fit is unclear.
- A disclosure needs follow-up but is not automatically disqualifying.

Reviewer note should say exactly what is missing or what follow-up is needed.

## Reject cases

Reject when the reviewer sees:

- Spam, bot-like, or low-effort application behavior.
- Impersonation or obviously fake identity signals.
- No usable contact path after follow-up or review.
- Bad-faith intent, recruiting spam, scam patterns, or attempts to exploit the beta.
- Conflicts that would compromise review integrity or user safety.
- Requests for access that are clearly outside the purpose of Cred Bureau closed beta.

## Conflict and disclosure handling

Conflicts are not automatic rejection.

Reviewers should reject only when the conflict creates a meaningful trust, safety, or review-integrity problem. Otherwise, keep the application pending with notes or approve with the conflict captured in the Decision Log.

## Manual decision record

For every approval or rejection:

1. Add concise reviewer notes.
2. Set status to approved or rejected manually.
3. Confirm the Decision Log entry is created.
4. If approved, Jim manually adds the applicant to the group.
5. Close the review box after the manual add or rejection is complete.
