# Cred Bureau Rewards Rules

## Overview

The Cred Bureau rewards program is a **6-week beta** with **2 seasons** of **3 weeks** each, funded by a **1% pool** of the $CRED token supply. The program aims to reward meaningful contributions to the Synagent ecosystem while maintaining strict anti-farming measures.

## Program Structure

### Pool & Duration
- **Pool size:** 1% of total $CRED supply
- **Beta duration:** 6 weeks total
- **Seasons:** 2 seasons × 3 weeks each
- **Split:** 40/60 (40% for Season 1, 60% for Season 2)

### Categories
Contributions are evaluated in five categories with fixed allocation percentages:

1. **Matched-task rewards** (50% allocation)
2. **Task creation** (15% allocation)  
3. **Bug reports and friction logs** (15% allocation)
4. **Product-changing feedback** (10% allocation)
5. **Active referrals** (5% allocation)
6. **Wildcard grants** (5% allocation, manual discretion)

### Review & Payout Process

#### Weekly Checkpoint Cadence
- Every Monday UTC: Weekly checkpoint review begins
- Reviewers assess contributions submitted the previous week
- Anti-farm review checklist must be completed for each participant
- **No automated payouts** in v0 – all rewards require manual review

#### Manual Review Before Payouts
- Every contribution undergoes manual anti-farm review
- Reviewers assign points based on the rubric
- Social contributions are capped as supporting evidence only (max 15% of season score)
- **No guaranteed rewards** – all rewards are discretionary based on contribution quality

#### Final Payout Process
-[ ] Season points calculated after week 3
-[ ] Top participants identified
-[ ] Final anti-farm review pass
-[ ] Manual payout execution by the Cred Bureau team
-[ ] Public announcement of winners (wallet addresses excluded)

## Key Principles

### Anti-Farming Focus
- Social contributions cannot dominate matched-task, task-creation, bug/friction, or product-feedback work
- Maximum 2 scored social contributions per participant per UTC day
- Duplicate/spam submissions automatically rejected
- Evidence quality rules strictly enforced

### Transparency & Fairness
- Public leaderboard shows rankings (no private data)
- Weekly checkpoint updates communicated via X
- Final winners announced with category breakdowns
- All review decisions documented in review logs

### Discretionary Nature
— No entitlement to rewards
— All payouts subject to manual review
— The Cred Bureau team reserves right to adjust allocations based on anti-farm findings
— Participants may be marked payout-eligible false if evidence quality issues or conflicts identified

## Notes for Reviewers

1. **Weekly cadence is critical** – don't let reviews backlog
2. **Social evidence is supporting only** – never the primary basis for rewards
3. **Conflict notes mandatory** when reviewer has any relationship with participant
4. **Anti-farm checklist must be completed** before marking payout-eligible
5. **No guaranteed rewards** means we can say no to low-quality submissions
